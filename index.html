THE

GRUG-BRAINED MARKETER

MANIFESTO

Marketing got complicated somewhere along the way. Between the 47-step growth playbooks, the synergy-driven frameworks, and LinkedIn gurus shilling curiosity gaps, we forgot what we were actually doing.

Helping people find things that help them.

This is for marketers who are tired. Tired of jargon, tired of complexity theatre, tired of pretending that a 14-touchpoint nurture sequence is somehow better than just saying what you mean to people who care.

These are twelve rules. They won't make you a marketing genius. But they might remind you that coffee and common sense solve more problems than any framework ever will.

01

Complexity is the enemy

In strategy, campaigns, and the words you use. That campaign with 14 touchpoints and 6 segments that nobody can maintain? Kill it.

02

Know your one thing. Protect it.

The CEO wants TikTok. Sales wants a one-pager. Events needs a banner. You still need to ship the landing page.

03

Don't be a marketing shaman

No dogma. ABM isn't magic. Inbound vs outbound is a false war. The framework that works is the one you actually execute.

04

Ship before it's perfect

That blog post sitting in draft for three weeks because you're not happy with the intro? It isn't helping anyone.

05

Give value before you ask

Teach, share, help—then ask for the demo. Not the other way around.

06

Measure what you need to know

300 webinar signups mean nothing if zero are ICP. A "failed" campaign with 12 signups that produced 3 SQLs? That's a win.

07

Learn by doing

You won't learn SEO from a course. You'll learn it by publishing 50 posts and watching what ranks.

08

Respect what came before you

That "ugly" email sequence your predecessor built might have been tuned through 50 A/B tests you don't know about.

09

Share everything, hoard nothing

Your playbooks, templates, tools—give them away. The community you build is worth more than hoarding a Google Sheet.

10

Talk simple

If your campaign brief uses the word "synergy," start over.

11

Everyone's an imposter, keep moving

Marketing isn't formulaic. Feedback loops are vague. Nobody actually knows what they're doing. That's fine. Keep moving.

12

Experiment always

Don't guess. Test. The best way to learn is doing it wrong first. The second best way is doing it wrong again, slightly differently.
